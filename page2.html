Background of the Novel

Noli Me Tangere, the translation of "Touch Me Not" in Latin, was written by Jose Rizal, one of the country's most significant heroes in history. Noli Me Tangere, along with El Filibusterismo, were the two novels of Rizal that were used to combat against the Spaniards during the time of the Spanish Colonization. The author started writing this novel in 1884, and he finished writing it in 1886. It was first published in Berlin the year after. The original copy was written in Spanish, and a number of authors have created versions of this novel in English and Filipino.

The historical novel is relevant to this day, as it is being taught in several schools. It demonstrates to people what the Filipinos went through during the Spanish period. It also makes people aware and educated about the various social, political, and moral issues that the world faces today. Each character in the novel symbolizes a certain issue that is relevant today.



Primary Characters in the Novel

NOLI ME TANGERE CHARACTERS
APPEARANCE NAME AND ALIAS DESCRIPTION
Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin (Crisostomo Ibarra)
  • Crisostomo Ibarra is a young and wealthy mestizo who came home after 7 years of studying to find out what happened to his father.
  • He is the only child of Don Rafael Ibarra and the grandchild of Don Saturnino Ibarra.
  • He is an intelligent and respectful person.
Maria Clara de los Santos y Alba (Maria Clara)
  • Maria Clara is the only child of Kapitan Tiago and Doña Pia Alba.
  • She is also Ibarra's childhood sweetheart and fiancee.
  • In the later part of the story, it was revealed that Padre Damaso is her biological father.
Damaso Verdolagas (Padre Damaso)
  • Padre Damaso is a Franciscan priest who once served as the curate of San Diego for almost two decades. He was then replaced by Padre Salvi.
  • Throughout the story, he was shown as a ruthless, arrogant, and hot-headed person.
  • He was the godfather of Maria Clara, until it was revealed that he was her biological father.
  • In the early chapters, he ordered a gravedigger to bring Don Rafael's corpse to the Chinese cemetery.
Bernardo Salvi (Padre Salvi)
  • Padre Salvi is the priest who took over Padre Damaso's role in San Diego.
  • He is called a "dead fly" due to his weak and sickly body.
  • He punishes people by fining them money for his own benefit.
  • He is known to have bad terms with the Alferez.
Hernando de la Sibyla (Padre Sibyla)
  • Padre Sibyla is a Dominican priest who became the friar of Binondo.
  • He became a professor in San Juan de Letran.
  • He is usually a quiet and short-spoken person.
Don Santiago de los Santos (Kapitan Tiago/Don Tiago)
  • Kapitan Tiago is one of the richest men in Binondo. He owns land in San Diego, Pampanga, and Laguna.
  • He is the father of Maria Clara, the husband of Doña Pia Alba, and the cousin of Tiya Isabel.
  • He is a religious person; his house is surrounded with sacred relics and objects. He also shows how religious he is by offering huge amounts to the church.
  • In the beginning of the story, he arranges a gathering in his house for Ibarra's arrival.
Isabel Alba (Tiya Isabel)
  • Tiya Isabel is the cousin of Kapitan Tiago and the aunt of Maria Clara.
  • Due to Doña Pia Alba's death at childbirth, Tiya Isabel became the caretaker and mother figure of Maria Clara.
Miguel Guevarra (Tinyente Guevarra)
  • Tinyente Guevarra is one of the lieutenants of the Guardia Sibil.
  • After the gathering, he informed Ibarra about what happened to his father.
  • According to him, he is a friend of Don Rafael. He had tried his best to clear Don Rafael's name, but before he did, Don Rafael had died in prison.
Anastacio (Pilosopo Tasyo/Mang Tasyo/Baliw)
  • Pilosopo Tasyo is a wise and secular philosopher.
  • He studied at San Colegio de San Juan, but he was forced to quit by his mother in fear that he would stop believing Catholic beliefs.
  • When his wife died, he spent his time and wealth reading books and studying.
  • Due to his knowledge, uneducated people viewed him as a crazy man.
Narcisa (Sisa)
  • Sisa is the mother of Basilio and Crispin and the wife of Pedro.
  • She is a victim of abuse from her husband. Despite being desperate to protect her children, she was not able to do anything to protect them, causing her to lose her sanity.
Basilio
  • Basilio is the 10-year-old child of Sisa and Pedro and the older brother of Crispin.
  • He is a brave and strong child who tries to protect his brother. He wishes to protect his family from the environment they are in.
Crispin
  • Crispin is the 7-year-old child of Sisa and Pedro and the younger brother of Crispin.
  • He is a soft-hearted and fearful kid and relies on Basilio to protect him.
  • He was falsely accused of stealing 2 onsa (32 pesos), and he was punished for it. He ends up going missing and, most likely, dead.
